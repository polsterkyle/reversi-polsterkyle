An Ode To Manatees

Herbivorous and dull,
their form signals fear
yet they quiver in benevolence

Where green moss waves along the dock
and gray orbs float and sway
Their lips, like fingers,
pluck previously proper bristle

Their coarse skin meets the humid air
and the salted lichens arise together
to glean the virtue of symbiosis

Some days I imagine times
where cabbage leaves glint like gold
how the purr and spin of boats never tarnished
their backs nor biomes
